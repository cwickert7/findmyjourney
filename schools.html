Find My Journey for secondary schools

Find My Journey is designed to complement your existing careers program — not replace it. It gives students a structured, psychologically grounded starting point for career conversations that your pathways advisors can build on.

Unlike traditional aptitude-based tools, Find My Journey asks who a student is, not what they can do on a timed test. This makes it particularly effective for students who don’t perform well under pressure — including neurodiverse students, school refusers, and those with anxiety.

How schools use Find My Journey

📋
Year 9 & 10 subject selection
Help students choose senior subjects with a clearer sense of direction. Find My Journey surfaces values and interests that inform subject choice conversations.
🎓
Year 11 & 12 career planning
Give senior students a personalised career map they can bring into pathways advisor appointments. Find My Journey includes ATAR and non-ATAR pathways, apprenticeships and TAFE routes.
🧠
VCE VM and applied learning
Find My Journey is particularly well-suited to VCE VM cohorts. The assessment is conversational, not test-based, and the results include practical, work-ready pathways alongside further study options.
🤝
Student wellbeing integration
The Sensitive Explorer Protocol identifies students who need calm, structured environments — without requiring disclosure. Results for these students are filtered to avoid overwhelming or competitive settings.

Methodology

Find My Journey is grounded in established career development theory, including Holland’s RIASEC model, Super’s life-span theory and Savickas’s career construction approach. The assessment uses ipsative measurement (forced-choice pairs) to reduce social desirability bias — students answer what feels true, not what sounds impressive.

The tool differentiates itself through its AI personalisation layer — every result is written specifically for the student, referencing what they actually told us — and through its explicit design for neurodiverse and disengaged young people.

Pilot program

We are currently offering pilot access to a small number of Australian secondary schools. Pilot schools receive pilot access for their student cohort, a debrief with our team, and the opportunity to shape the product roadmap.
